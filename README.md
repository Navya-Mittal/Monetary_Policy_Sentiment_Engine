# ECB Speech Sentiment — Euronext Dublin Rate-Sensitive Signal Engine

A quantitative research tool that parses ECB Governing Council speeches for hawkish/dovish monetary policy sentiment and maps directional signals onto Irish rate-sensitive equities listed on Euronext Dublin.

## Overview

Conventional equity analysis often underweights the forward guidance embedded in central bank communication. This project builds a domain-specific sentiment engine — grounded in the Picault & Renault (2017) lexicon methodology — that scores ECB speeches on a hawkish/dovish axis and constructs a sector rotation signal across Irish banks, REITs, and industrials.

The core thesis: hawkish ECB language predicts near-term underperformance in rate-sensitive sectors (REITs, utilities) and outperformance in banks (net interest margin expansion), with statistically significant correlation at 5–20 day horizons.

## Methodology

### Sentiment Scoring
- **Lexicon:** Domain-specific hawkish/dovish word lists derived from Apel & Blix Grimaldi (2012) and Picault & Renault (2017)
- **Negation handling:** Negated hawkish terms (`"not tightening"`) are treated as dovish signals
- **Intensity weighting:** Strong-signal terms weighted 2× moderate terms
- **Output:** Net score normalised to `[-1, +1]`; `+1` = extremely hawkish, `-1` = extremely dovish

### Signal Construction
- Forward return correlation computed at 1, 5, 10, and 20 trading day horizons
- Sector rotation rebalanced on each ECB speech date with a 10-day holding period

### Strategy Logic
| ECB Stance | Action |
|---|---|
| Hawkish | Overweight banks (AIB, BOI), underweight REITs |
| Dovish | Overweight REITs (IRES, HRN), underweight banks |
| Neutral | Equal weight across all tickers |
